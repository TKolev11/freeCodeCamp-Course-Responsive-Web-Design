John Vincent Atanasoff

The man who started an era

John Atansoff is the man who created the first computer

Here's a timeline of John Atanasoff's life:

  • 1903 - Born in Hamilton, New York.
  • 1921 - Graduates from high school in Florida.
  • 1925 - Earns a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the University of Florida.
  • 1926 - Pursues graduate studies at Iowa State College (now Iowa State University).
  • 1930 - Completes his Ph.D. in theoretical physics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
  • 1930 - 1937 - Works as a professor at Iowa State College, specializing in mathematics and physics.
  • 1937 - Collaborates with graduate student Clifford Berry to conceptualize and build the Atanasoff-Berry Computer (ABC), an electronic digital computing device.
  • 1939 - Faces challenges in completing the ABC due to World War II and other priorities.
  • 1942 - - Leaves Iowa State College to contribute to war efforts, working on naval defense projects
  • 1948 - Legal disputes arise over the patent for the electronic digital computer, involving Atanasoff and John Mauchly, a developer of the ENIAC computer.
  • 1973 - The court rules that Atanasoff's ABC design was the first electronic digital computer, overturning Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert's ENIAC patent.
  • 1975 - Receives the Computer Pioneer Award from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).
  • 1990 - Awarded the National Medal of Technology by President George H.W. Bush.
  • 1991 - Inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.
  • 1997 - Honored with the establishment of the John Atanasoff Award for outstanding achievements in the field of computer science or information technology.
  • 2002 - Passes away at the age of 91 in Maryland, leaving a lasting legacy in the development of the electronic digital computer.
